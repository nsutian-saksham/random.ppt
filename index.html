Background

Unemployment and economic growth are the two most critical macroeconomic indicators for any developing economy. This study focuses on India, one of the world's fastest-growing economies, to analyze how changes in GDP growth affect unemployment rates.

Key Insight
GDP growth and unemployment are inversely related according to Okun's Law
Theoretical Basis

Okun's Law posits an inverse relationship between GDP growth and unemployment. When output increases, firms hire more workers, leading to lower unemployment rates.

Econometric Analysis
Okun's Law Theory

Okun's Law suggests an inverse relationship between GDP growth and unemployment. When output increases, firms hire more workers, leading to lower unemployment rates.

U_t = β₀ + β₁·GDP_t + u_t
Where U = Unemployment rate, GDP = GDP growth rate
Research Objectives
1
To examine the growth-unemployment nexus in India (1998-2023)
2
To estimate the impact using OLS and evaluate statistical significance
3
To check model assumptions and improve specification using COVID dummy

Research Limitations
Informal Sector: Unemployment data may not fully capture India's large informal sector dynamics, potentially understating true unemployment levels.
Small Sample: Only 26 observations (1998-2023) with limited degrees of freedom for robust statistical inference.
Omitted Variables: Education levels, policy shifts, and sectoral composition not included in the model.
Measurement Issues: Potential inconsistencies in macroeconomic indicator definitions over time.
Results pass basic checks but should be interpreted with caution
